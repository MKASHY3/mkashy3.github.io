A closer look: how poorer economies rank on total vaccinations vs. covid cases

Try hovering over some countries and you should see the total vaccinations per hundred, along with the total covid cases per million:

  • Here we take a closer look at the same dataset as the previous chart (Slide 1) but examine only countries with minimal number of covid cases per million people as well as minimal number of vaccinations per hundred people.
  • This drilled-down POV on the previous dataset provides the insight that for many countries who were impacted by the coronavirus, a lower number of covid cases coincided with a similarly low number of vaccinations received.
  • Something to consider, however, is that many of the countries identified in this display are also linked to a lower socioeconomic status, which may have contributed to the low vaccination rates (a simple lack of supply of vaccines).
  • Finally, the lower number of coronavirus cases reported may have also been due to a lack of accurate reporting and recording procedures to maintain track of the true number of cases occurring in the particular country. The numbers seen here may not be entirely accurate therefore and do not tell the full story necessarily.
